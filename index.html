Switch this simulation to your dedicated GPU

Your browser is rendering on the integrated Intel GPU. Laptops with hybrid graphics (Intel + NVIDIA) often force the browser onto the weaker chip. To use your RTX 4060:

  1. Plug in & set Windows power mode to Best performance.
  2. Settings → System → Display → Graphics → add your browser → Options → High performance → Save.
  3. NVIDIA Control Panel → Manage 3D settings → Program Settings → your browser → High-performance NVIDIA processor.
  4. Fully quit & reopen the browser, reload. Verify at chrome://gpu — it should say NVIDIA.

Full guide: docs/USE_NVIDIA_GPU.md. The performance badge turns green once a dedicated GPU is detected.

Welcome to Black Hole Explorer

A real-time, scientifically accurate black-hole simulator. Light is traced through curved spacetime — what you see is genuine gravitational lensing.

Get around

  • Drag to orbit · scroll to zoom
  • Click the scene (or T) to throw matter in
  • C cinematic · R reset · H panel

Try this first

  • Pick a real hole in Black hole → preset (Sgr A*, M87*…)
  • Watch the Live readouts update in real units
  • Open Geometry → Photon paths to see light bend
  • Tap 📖 Learn for the science, or any ⓘ

On a gaming laptop? If the badge says Integrated GPU, switch the browser to your dedicated card for full quality (see the banner / docs/USE_NVIDIA_GPU.md).

WebGL2 is required

This simulation needs a browser with WebGL2 (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari 15+) and hardware acceleration enabled.
